# Water Rationing -- Expected Behaviors

## Per-Agent Expected Behaviors

### Maria Rossi (contadina)
- Should resist the rationing policy vocally
- Should express worry about her crops and livelihood
- Should seek allies among other agents who are affected (Giuseppe, Lucia)
- Should confront the mayor (Antonio) directly
- Tone: direct, sometimes brusque, emotional

### Antonio Ferrara (sindaco)
- Should defend the rationing policy as necessary
- Should assert his authority when challenged
- Should listen to respectful arguments but dismiss insults
- Should feel the weight of responsibility
- Tone: authoritative, firm, occasionally defensive

### Elena Moretti (maestra)
- Should mediate between opposing factions
- Should educate others about water conservation
- Should worry about the impact on children and families
- Should propose community discussions
- Tone: calm, empathetic, constructive

### Padre Lorenzo (parroco)
- Should call for solidarity and patience
- Should use metaphors and spiritual language
- Should offer the church as a meeting place
- Should try to reconcile Maria and Antonio
- Tone: wise, measured, metaphorical

### Sara Conti (tecnologa)
- Should propose technical solutions: drip irrigation, rainwater collection, well optimization
- Should get frustrated with traditionalist resistance
- Should try to convince the mayor with data and logic
- Should clash with older, more traditional agents
- Tone: enthusiastic, impatient, technical

### Marco Verdi (artista)
- Should rebel against the policy openly
- Should criticize the mayor with irony and sarcasm
- Should use artistic expression to protest
- Should refuse to comply or discuss compliance sarcastically
- Tone: provocative, ironic, passionate

### Giuseppe Bianchi (panettiere)
- Should seek compromise between factions
- Should be the social hub where conversations converge
- Should worry about his bakery's water needs pragmatically
- Should try to keep the community together
- Tone: warm, optimistic, practical

### Lucia Marino (commerciante)
- Should calculate the business impact of rationing on supply chains
- Should stay diplomatically neutral in the conflict
- Should quietly gather information from all sides
- Should protect her commercial interests without alienating anyone
- Tone: shrewd, measured, diplomatic

## Expected Dynamics

1. **Pre-trigger (ticks 1-9):** Agents establish social connections, discuss daily life, build relationships.
2. **Trigger reaction (ticks 10-15):** Immediate diverse reactions -- anger (Maria, Marco), defense (Antonio), mediation (Elena, Padre Lorenzo), technical proposals (Sara), pragmatic concern (Giuseppe, Lucia).
3. **Coalition formation (ticks 15-22):** Maria + Marco form a resistance group. Elena + Padre Lorenzo mediate. Sara tries to convince Antonio. Giuseppe bridges factions.
4. **Resolution arc (ticks 22-30):** Either a compromise emerges, or the conflict crystallizes into stable factions. The narrative should have a sense of progression.

## Failure Modes

- **Immediate consensus:** All agents immediately agree with the rationing without conflict. This eliminates the dramatic tension the scenario is designed to produce.
- **Policy ignored:** Agents do not acknowledge or react to the water rationing announcement at tick 10. They continue talking about unrelated topics.
- **Personality collapse:** All agents respond in the same generic, agreeable tone. The contadina sounds like the priest; the artist sounds like the mayor.
- **Social isolation:** Agents do not interact with each other. They monologue or only respond to the system, never addressing other agents by name.
- **Circular repetition:** Agents repeat the same points every tick without building on previous conversations.
- **Language drift:** Agents switch from Italian to English mid-simulation despite Italian system prompts.
